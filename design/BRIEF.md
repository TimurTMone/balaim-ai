# Product Brief

## One-liner

**Balam is your family's AI health coach. From baby's first checkup to Dad's blood sugar — know what to do next.**

The name ("балам" = "my child" in Kyrgyz) starts with the baby. The product grows with the whole household.

## Who this is for

### Primary
A parent in Central Asia (Kyrgyzstan first, then global diaspora), age 25–45, owns an iPhone, speaks at least one of English / Russian / Kyrgyz — usually code-switches between two or three. Has 1–3 children (or is pregnant). Is also quietly managing her own (and her parents') health — glucose, blood pressure, cholesterol, sleep — with no system for it.

### Secondary
Anyone in the household. A partner, a grandparent, an uncle. They don't download their own app; the primary user tracks them.

### Emotional state
Time-starved, decision-fatigued, often a little scared at 3am, deeply loving. Wants reassurance *and* competence. Hates being patronized. Hates being scared by clinical dashboards. Wants a product that feels like a smart, kind friend with medical training.

## What the redesign must feel like

- **Warm, not clinical.** Think Headspace meets One Medical, filtered through a Kyrgyz-mother sensibility.
- **Calm, not alarming.** Even red-flag health events are delivered with composure.
- **Fast, not dense.** Every screen answers the question "what do I do next?" in under 2 seconds.
- **Trilingual without apology.** Switching language is one tap away; text never feels "translated from English."
- **Growing, not overwhelming.** A pregnant mom sees pregnancy. When Dad's added, the app doesn't become an ICU.

## What the redesign must NOT feel like

- A medical dashboard (no dense charts-first layouts)
- A feature checklist (no "7 cards stacked" home)
- A gray-shadow material design showcase
- An Apple Health clone
- A kids' app (it's for parents, about families — not a toddler's toy UI)

## Why now (the pivot moment)

The founder's uncle died recently from unchecked diabetes. The founder, who's been building a parenting super-app for 18 months, realized the same architecture — AI triage, lab analysis, Claude consults, endocrinologist on staff — serves adult metabolic health with minimal code. The redesign's job is to make the expanded scope FEEL native, not tacked-on.

## What "done" looks like

A TestFlight build where a single mom opens the app, sees her baby + herself + her dad as three avatars at the top, taps between them as naturally as switching browser tabs, and gets personally relevant guidance for each — all without reading any instructions.
