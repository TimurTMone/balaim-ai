### 10. Newborn — First week home

>

> What you can do:
> → Skin-to-skin, hours a day
> → Feed on demand (every 2-3 hours)

> → Accept ALL help
>
> You cannot spoil a newborn. Respond to every cry.

## TIPS SERIES

### 17. The 5 S's for soothing
> Every newborn parent should know Dr. Harvey Karp's 5 S's:
>
> S — Swaddle (snug arms, loose hips)
> S — Side or stomach (IN YOUR ARMS only — always on back to sleep)
> S — Shush (loud white noise)
> S — Swing (tiny jiggles)
> S — Suck (pacifier or breast)
>
> Do them TOGETHER. Not one at a time.
>
> Most crying babies calm in 60 seconds.
>
> #newbornsoothing #5Ss #newparent

---

### 18. Is it normal at 24 weeks?
> Things that are 100% normal at 24 weeks but sound scary:
>
> • Braxton Hicks (practice contractions)
> • Linea nigra (dark line on belly)
> • Swollen ankles
> • Round ligament pain
> • Congestion
> • Vivid dreams
>
> Things that are NOT normal:
> ⚠️ Regular, painful contractions
> ⚠️ Bleeding
> ⚠️ Severe swelling in face/hands
> ⚠️ Reduced fetal movement
>
> When in doubt, call your doctor. Always.
>
> #week24pregnant #pregnancysymptoms #isitnormal

### 20. Kick counts — why they matter
> After week 28, do kick counts daily.
>
> The rule: 10 kicks in 2 hours.
>
> Best time: after a meal, lying on your side.
>
> If you notice:
> ⚠️ A significant decrease in movement
> ⚠️ No kicks in 2 hours (after trying cold water + food)
>
> Call your doctor or go to labor & delivery. Better to check than wait.
>
> Kick counts save lives.
>
> #kickcounts #thirdtrimester #pregnancysafety

## MYTH-BUSTING SERIES

### 21. Pregnancy myths busted
> Things grandma said that aren't true:
>
> ❌ Raising your arms overhead wraps the cord
> ❌ Spicy food brings on labor (it doesn't)
> ❌ You need to eat for two (you need ~300 extra calories)
> ❌ Heartburn = hairy baby (not reliably)
> ❌ You can't exercise when pregnant (most people should)
>
> ✅ What's true: every pregnancy is different. Your doctor knows your situation.
>
> #pregnancymyths #pregnancyfacts #oldwivestales

---

### 22. Sleep myths for babies
> Baby sleep myths that need to die:
>
> ❌ "Adding rice cereal helps baby sleep longer" (false AND a choking risk)
> ❌ "You can't hold baby too much" (wait, this one is TRUE)
> ❌ "Letting them cry it out is cruel" (research doesn't support this)
> ❌ "They'll outgrow it" (sometimes they do, sometimes they need help)
>
> What helps: consistent bedtime, dark room, white noise, age-appropriate routine.
>
> #babysleep #newparent #parentingtips

---

### 23. When to worry vs when not to
> 6-month-old red flags (call the doctor if your baby):
>
> ⚠️ Cannot sit with support
> ⚠️ Does not reach for objects
> ⚠️ Does not respond to sounds or name
> ⚠️ Does not show affection to familiar people
> ⚠️ Does not babble
>
> Everything else? Likely normal variation.
>
> Babies develop on their own timeline. The 9-15 month walking range is NORMAL.
>
> Trust your gut. If something feels off, ask.
>
> #babydevelopment #redflags #pediatrician
