## Thread 1: "Things no one tells you about the first week with a newborn"

**1/**
Things no one told me about the first week home with a newborn.

A thread that would have saved me panic 🧵

**2/**
Your baby will lose 5-10% of their birth weight.

This is NORMAL. They regain it by day 10-14.

The nurses won't always explain this. It terrified me.

**3/**
Newborns sleep 14-17 hours a day, but in 2-4 hour stretches around the clock.

Day and night are the same to them. No amount of routine will fix this yet.

Sleep when they sleep. Seriously.

**4/**
Colostrum (that first thick, yellow milk) is called "liquid gold" for a reason.

Your body produces ONLY 1-2 teaspoons per feed in the first 2 days.

That's enough. Your baby's stomach is the size of a marble.

**5/**
The 2am wake-up is universal.

Cluster feeding in the evening/night = building milk supply. It's not a problem. It's biology.

**6/**
You will Google "is this normal" 47 times in week one.

Things that ARE normal:
- Breasts leaking randomly
- Baby sleeping with their eyes half open
- Weird newborn rashes
- Feeling completely overwhelmed

**7/**
Things that are NOT normal (go to ER):
- Rectal temp over 38°C
- Not feeding / floppy baby
- Yellow skin getting worse
- Difficulty breathing

Trust your gut. ERs exist for this.

**8/**
You cannot spoil a newborn.

Respond to every cry. Hold them as much as you want. Skin-to-skin for hours.

"You'll spoil them" is an old wives' tale. Attachment science says the opposite.

**9/**
Postpartum emotions are intense and non-linear.

Baby blues (days 1-14): crying, mood swings — hormonal, normal.
Postpartum depression (past 2 weeks): needs support.

Talk to your doctor. No shame.

**10/**
The first week is survival mode.

Accept all help. Say no to visitors. Eat the casseroles.

You're doing the hardest thing you've ever done. You're doing great.

## Thread 2: "Pregnancy week 24 — your baby can hear you now"

**1/**
Week 24 of pregnancy. Your baby can now hear your voice. 🧵

Here's why this moment changes everything:

**2/**
At 24 weeks, the cochlea and auditory nerve are fully developed.

Baby hears sounds through amniotic fluid — your heartbeat, your digestion, your voice.

**3/**
They prefer YOUR voice above all others.

Studies show babies born to moms who talked/sang during pregnancy can distinguish their mom's voice from strangers' within days of birth.

**4/**
They also remember music.

A 2009 study: babies whose moms played a specific melody in the 3rd trimester stopped crying at birth when that melody was played again.

**5/**
What this means for you:

Talk to your bump. Narrate your day. Read a book out loud. Sing (badly is fine).

You're building the soundtrack of their life before they're born.

**6/**
Dads/partners — this is your invitation.

Baby will recognize YOUR voice too if you talk to them now. This is bonding that doesn't wait for birth.

**7/**
Also at week 24:
✓ Baby develops taste buds
✓ Lungs are developing surfactant (crucial for breathing)
✓ Sleep cycles begin
✓ Brain grows rapidly

**8/**
Physical changes for you:
- Braxton Hicks contractions
- Linea nigra darkening
- Swollen ankles
- Glucose screening test is coming up (24-28 weeks)

## Thread 4: "The 0-24 month development checklist doctors don't give you"

**1/**
Your pediatrician has 15 minutes per visit.

Here's the developmental checklist they don't have time to give you. 0-24 months. 🧵

**2/**
**Month 1-2:**
✓ Lifts head during tummy time
✓ Follows objects with eyes
✓ First social smile (4-6 weeks)
✓ Coos and makes vowel sounds

**3/**
**Month 4:**
✓ Rolls tummy to back
✓ Laughs out loud
✓ Pushes up on extended arms
✓ Responds to name
✓ Babbles chains of consonants

**4/**
**Month 6:**
✓ Sits with minimal support
✓ Babbles distinct syllables
✓ Begins solid foods
✓ Stranger anxiety starts
✓ Looks for dropped objects

**5/**
**Month 9:**
✓ Crawls (any style counts)
✓ Pulls to standing
✓ Says "mama/dada" (non-specific)
✓ Waves bye-bye
✓ Points at objects

**6/**
**Month 12:**
✓ Stands independently
✓ Uses 1-5 words meaningfully
✓ Takes first steps (9-15 months is normal)
✓ Drinks from a cup
✓ Imitates actions

**7/**
**Month 15-18:**
✓ Walks confidently
✓ Uses 10-50 words
✓ Combines 2 words
✓ Follows simple instructions
✓ Shows empathy

**8/**
**Month 24:**
✓ Runs
✓ Jumps with both feet
✓ Uses 200-300 words
✓ 2-3 word sentences
✓ Engages in pretend play

**9/**

## Thread 5: "Things partners can do in each trimester"

**1/**
Partners/dads. Pregnancy isn't just happening to her. It's happening to YOU both.

Here's what to do in each trimester. 🧵

**2/**
**First trimester (weeks 1-13):**

She's exhausted and nauseous. Her hormones are wild.

✓ Take over grocery runs (smells trigger nausea)
✓ Keep snacks everywhere
✓ Do the dishes — every night
✓ Don't take mood swings personally
✓ Come to the first ultrasound

**3/**
**Second trimester (weeks 14-27):**

The "good" trimester. Energy's back. Belly's growing.

✓ Come to the anatomy scan (week 20)
✓ Start talking to the bump (baby can hear you at 24 weeks)
✓ Book a babymoon if possible
✓ Start researching car seats together

**4/**
**Third trimester (weeks 28-40):**

She's uncomfortable, anxious, and nesting.

✓ Take on MORE chores
✓ Foot and back massages (she's carrying 25+ lbs of baby)
✓ Tour the hospital together
✓ Pack YOUR own hospital bag
✓ Install the car seat

**5/**
**Day of birth:**

You're not "helping." You're co-parenting from second one.

✓ Skin-to-skin with baby after the birth
✓ Change the first diaper
✓ Advocate for her (she'll be exhausted)
✓ Take photos — but put the phone down

**6/**
**First 6 weeks postpartum:**

She's healing from a medical event. Don't expect her to cook.

✓ Take over nighttime diapers
✓ Handle burping and settling
✓ Bring her water/snacks while she nurses
✓ Field visitors
✓ Tell her she's doing great (daily)

**7/**
The best gift you can give your child:
A partnership where BOTH parents are present.

Starting in pregnancy. Not after birth.
